THE Mary Health AI STORY

PROBLEM

About half of the worldwide population lacks access to essential health services. Billions of people living in low-and-middle income countries have to travel for over two hours to reach medical facilities (WHO)

STATISTICS 83% of the rural population in Sub-Saharan Africa lives more than two hours away from the nearest hospital. 800 million people spend at least 10% of their household budgets on health expenses with ~100 million pushed into extreme poverty each year due to these costs. Many LMICs have as few as 1 doctor per 10,000 people.

OUR SOLUTION

Like M-PESA or MTN Mobile Money did for financial services, or like IBM innovated the infrastructure that propelled the personal computing industry, we are bringing quality healthcare closer to people's homes with micro-clinics operated by medical professionals who are empowered with AI and portable diagnostic tools to provide primary care and secondary prevention of disease.

Our approach is simple

MaryHealth micro-clinics are modular and can be transported to any part of the world. They provide the physical space for patients to interact with care providers. Care providers have access to our software, AI models, medical equipment, supplies, and the technology needed to provide primary care and secondary prevention of diseases.
